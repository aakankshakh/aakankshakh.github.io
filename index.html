Intro

Hi there! I'm currently an undergraduate student at Mount Allison University completing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and Mathematics with a minor in Biochemistry. I'm overall interested in the ethical implications of using artificial intelligence and machine learning in the healthcare domain. If you'd like to know more, check out some of the work I've done!

Work

Currently, I'm working on my honours thesis in Computer Science, where I am developing a model to detect cybersecurity attacks in a Healthcare Internet of Things (IoT). In particular, we are using machine learning techniques and automaton-based modelling. The model will have two responsibilities: authenticate new, unknown devices onto the network and monitor the IoT network to detect attacks.

I was also a summer research intern at The Simpson Lab developing a Responsible AI podcast (coming out soon!), researching capabilities of LLMs in healthcare, and looking at non-western ethical frameworks for healthcare AI systems. I also presented my work on analyzing racial and gender bias in MIMIC-IV clinical notes at the Science Atlantic MSCS Conference and won first place for undergraduate computer science talks!

Previously, I've worked for Dr. Matthew Betti on mathematical epidemiology where I mathematically modelled and analyzed COVID-19 data in Canada using a modified SIR model and worked on modifying a mathematical ODE model of honeybee colonies to analyze the threshold for hygienic behaviour.

I've also had the opportunity to be a fellow at the AI4Good lab in 2022 and attend the Bumblekite machine learning summer school in healthcare and the biosciences in July 2023.

For an incomplete overview of some of the things I've done, check out my CV.
